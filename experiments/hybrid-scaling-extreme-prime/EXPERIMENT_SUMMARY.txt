HYPOTHESIS TESTED
================================================================================

prediction accuracy across 1200+ orders of magnitude, where relative error

factorization in cryptographic contexts.

================================================================================
INTERPRETATION
================================================================================

What Works:
  The Prime Number Theorem-based prediction framework demonstrates excellent
  convergence properties and extreme scale accuracy. The dual-adapter 
  architecture is sound for handling multiple precision regimes.

What Doesn't Work:
  The geometric resonance detection with golden ratio and Euler number phase
  modulation does NOT produce asymmetric signals between semiprime factors.
  This falsifies the claimed cryptographic application.

Implications:
  - PNT-based prime approximation is robust and scalable
  - No evidence of resonance-based factorization shortcuts
  - Standard GNFS remains state-of-the-art for factorization
  - Claims about "q-targeted attacks" are not supported

Future Work:
  - Test with larger semiprimes (256-426 bit range)
  - Implement full C adapter for performance validation
  - Test with actual prime tables instead of approximations
  - Explore alternative resonance detection methods
